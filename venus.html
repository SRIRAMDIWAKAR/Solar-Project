Venus

Venus is the second-closest to the Sun in our solar system, often referred to as Earth's "sister planet" due to size and composition. Here are the following key facts about Venus: 1. **Size and Composition**: With a diameter of around 7,521 miles (12,104 kilometers), Venus is roughly almost the same size as Earth. Composition is largely rocky with a metallic core and a silicate mantle. 2. **Atmosphere**: Venus has an extremely dense atmosphere, mostly carbon dioxide and nitrogen with some clouds of sulfuric acid. A runaway greenhouse effect causes average surface temperatures to be about 900 degrees Fahrenheit on Venus, making Venus the hottest planet in our solar system. 3. **Surface Features**: Venus surface features include large plains, some highlands, and many volcanoes. No liquid water bodies are present there but shows signs of active volcanism might be preserved. 4. **Rotation and Orbit**: Venus is also very slow in rotation on the axis, which is about 243 Earth days to complete one turn. Curiously, Venus rotates in the opposite direction of most planets, so that here, the Sun rises in the west and sets in the east; orbital period around the Sun is approximately 225 Earth days. 5. **Waterless**: Venus does not have a significant amount of waters. Any that once existed probably got evaporated due to high heat and pressure levels of Venus. 6. **Explored**: During the course of time, several spacecraft have explored Venus, one such is NASA's Magellan and Soviet Union's Venera missions, which have provided with a wealth of information regarding its atmosphere, surface, and geological features. 7. **Volcanic Activity**: More significant proof of strong volcanic activities in Venus can be indicated by numerous giant volcanoes and vast lava plains. Other scientists think Venus is still geologically active. 8. **Climate**: An extreme greenhouse effect that has made the atmospheric pressure on Venus reach 92 times higher than on Earth, leaving Venus's environment inhospitable for life like it exists today. Venus is an interesting topic to pursue research in atmospheric science and climate studies, as scientists can gain insight into the impacts of greenhouse gases and climate change.
